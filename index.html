You're Facing a Broken System

Living with Bipolar Disorder—or supporting someone who is—can feel overwhelming not because of personal failure, but because medical and legal systems are confusing and hard to navigate. When support is needed most, many are left feeling unheard and alone.

The Problem
Living with Bipolar Disorder—or supporting someone who is—often means facing overwhelming systems alone. Medical decisions are confusing, legal rights are unclear, and during critical moments, people are left feeling isolated and unheard. In times of crisis, many don’t know where to turn or who can speak up for them when they can’t.
